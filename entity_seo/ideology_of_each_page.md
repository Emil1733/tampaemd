1. The Homepage: The "Lucrative" Target
   The homepage is typically the most powerful page on your site. Its goal is to rank for your most competitive and profitable "money" keywords.

Target: High-intent, broad search terms (e.g., "Pest Control Manhattan" or "Plumber near me").

Role: It acts as the central hub that receives the "cumulative authority" from all the smaller, more specific sub-pages.

2. Service Pages: The "Expertise" Builders
   The goal of these pages is not necessarily to rank for massive traffic, but to prove to Google that you are a subject matter expert.

Target: Specific entities/sub-topics (e.g., a page dedicated just to "Bed Bugs," "Leaky Faucets," or "Inventory Forecasting").

Role: They provide a "Density of Authority." By having a page for every common problem or service in your niche, you demonstrate that your domain is a deep source of knowledge.

Instruction: The speaker emphasizes that you should not keyword stuff these. Instead, make them genuinely helpful to humans by answering specific problems.

3. Location Pages: The "Context" Anchors
   For local businesses, these pages show Google the geographic entities you belong to.

Target: Specific cities or neighborhoods (e.g., "Upper West Side" or "Brooklyn Heights").

Role: They signal to Google's knowledge graph that your business is physically and contextually relevant to the specific area you want to rank in.

Instruction: Avoid "fluff" or generic city history. Talk about the specific service problems unique to that location (e.g., why a certain neighborhood has a specific pest problem).

4. Use Case/ICP Pages: The "B2B Authority"
   For B2B or SAS companies, these pages target the specific problems of your "Ideal Customer Profile" (ICP).

Target: Industry-specific challenges (e.g., "Overstocking issues" for inventory software).

Role: They function similarly to service pages by addressing "engrams" (related concepts) that Google expects to see on a high-quality B2B site.

5. "Zero Search Volume" Pages: The "Authority Density" Secret
   A unique point the speaker makes is that some pages are designed to target keywords that tools say have zero search volume.

Goal: To show Google's algorithm that you haven't skipped any details.

Role: Even if no one clicks them, their existence in your sitemap and navigation helps the entire domain rank better by increasing your "Authority Density" score.

Summary of the "Network" Goal:
The ultimate goal of all these pages together is to lower your relationship score in Google’s systems. By linking all these highly related entities (Services + Locations + Common Problems) back to your homepage through the "Global Navigation" (header and footer), you prove you are the most relevant result for that market.
